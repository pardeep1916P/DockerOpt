# Docker Size Estimation Models Comparison

We evaluated five AI models to see how accurately they estimate Docker image sizes strictly from non-optimal and optimized Dockerfiles.

We tested realistic multi-stage builds using three distinct tech stacks and compared the AI estimates to the exact sizes generated by the `docker build` daemon.

## 🏆 Final Verdict & Recommendations

### 🥇 Best Original Size Predictor: `DeepSeek v3.2` & `GPT-5.2`
When predicting large, unoptimized base systems (Node, Python, general Ubuntu images), **DeepSeek v3.2 (19.6% dev)** and **GPT-5.2 (22.2% dev)** understand the size overhead of package managers and base OS layers much better than other models. Claude massively over-estimated the Rust image (+79%).

### 🥇 Best Optimized Size Predictor: `Grok-4` & `Claude 4.5 Sonnet`
All models share a major hallucination: they fail to understand that `FROM scratch` heavily dropping the entire filesystem leaves only a sub-10MB binary behind. 
- DeepSeek predicted a tiny Rust scratch binary would be 1097 MB (21840% error!). 
- **Grok-4** guessed the Go binary would be 14MB (vs 7MB real) and Rust would be 48MB (vs 5MB real), making it by far the most capable at understanding aggressive multi-stage container optimization. 
- **Claude 4.5 Sonnet** is a close second and remains historically excellent at predicting JS/Python size drops. 

If your backend is attempting to show users **Realistic Size Savings** from optimization:
- **Use Claude 4.5 Sonnet:** Extremely accurate on standard Node/Python environments. For instance, its Node estimates (1390 -> 272 MB) were nearly identical to the real output (1530 -> 296 MB).
- **Use Grok-4:** Fantastic at grasping `scratch` / multi-stage drops, providing the lowest overall error rate on optimized outputs.
